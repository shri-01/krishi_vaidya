Gray leaf spot

Reason of cause

Gray leaf spot (GLS) is a common fungal disease in the United States caused by the pathogen Cercospora zeae-maydis in corn. Disease development is favored by warm temperatures, 80°F or 27 °C; and high humidity, relative humidity of 90% or higher for 12 hours or more.

Management and Control of Gray leaf spot of Corn

  • Cultural Practices: Cercospora zeae-maydis overwinters in corn debris, so production practices such as tillage and crop rotation that reduce the amount corn residue on the surface will decrease the amount of primary inoculum.
    Crop rotation away from corn can reduce disease pressure, but multiple years may be necessary in no-till scenarios.
  • Hybrid Resistance: Planting hybrids with a high level of genetic resistance can help reduce the risk of yield loss due to gray leaf spot infection.
  • Fungicides: During the growing season, foliar fungicides can be used to manage gray leaf spot outbreaks.
    Farmers must consider the cost of the application and market value of their corn before determining if fungicides will be an economical solution to GLS.
